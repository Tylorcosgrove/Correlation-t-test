Pearson Correlation
When to use

Both variables are continuous and you want to know the strength and direction of the linear relationship between them.

Assumption checklist
  • Linear relationship (check scatterplot)
  • Both variables approximately normal (Shapiro-Wilk, p > .05)
  • Homoscedasticity — even spread around line (visual + Spearman if violated)
  • Independence — no repeated participants across groups
  • Both variables continuous (or one continuous, one dichotomous)

Independent Samples T-Test
When to use

One continuous DV and one dichotomous IV. You want to know if the mean of one group differs from the mean of another.

Assumption checklist
  • DV approximately normal in each group (Shapiro-Wilk, p > .05)
  • Homogeneity of variance — Levene's test, p > .05 (if violated, use Welch's)
  • Independence — different participants in each group
  • DV continuous, IV dichotomous
Effect size (Cohen's d)
dLabel
